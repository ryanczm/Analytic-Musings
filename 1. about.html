About

Hi, my name is Ryan and I’m a Year 3 student at NUS Business School majoring in Finance & Analytics. I shall be learning in the general direction of finance, programming, mathematics and documenting my projects and experiences here. I thus am going to try and embark on a self-learning journey, which can be difficult learning topics without a formal education, but the key is to figure out how best to overcome that.

I’m also trying to come up with a cognitive learning framework of sorts, in which we can distill the different types of cognitive “modes” of thinking to learn or interact with a particular field or subject. Hence, going on a learning journey allows me to “test” that framework, and see if it works! However, what if we could teach people to expand their set of “modes”. This idea involves metacognition, or being aware of one’s thought process, and one can derive the so called “ideal” thought process for a subject by observing the thought processes of experts or people skilled at that subject, and trying to reverse-engineer that thought process to capture it, then to test it yourself.
