Valuation (Rosenbaum)

Last updated: 20 Dec 2020 » Notes on valuation, Rosenbaum (Valuation, M&A, IPO, LBO process) » Link to notes


Progress:

Did this midway through Dec 2020, for half a week. Then I stopped to learn front-end to build this website.


Review:

Read Rosenbaum, took some notes. Skipped complete valuation details (just overall process), more focused on the insight on the actual M&A, IPO, LBO process. Involves many, many parties, procedures, documents and memorandums. Interesting vocabulary/acronyms as well (bake-offs, red herrings, greenshoes, CIMs, DAs, CAs, RFP). Would come back to this in-depth in the future when time permits.


Likes:

Super detailed & comprehensive. There’s many resources on the technical valuation process but not enough on the actual process of things like M&As & IPOs, which as mentioned, I learnt is a very time-constrained complex process with lots of parties doing this and that. Some of the terminologies can be really strange in a way (most memorable being the bakeoff, not the Great British Bake Off, but IBs presenting their IPO pitch to the IPO client who picks the syndicate).


Dislikes:

None


Learning points:

One thing that struck out was the ability to perceive the network of different entities or parties that have their own intentions and objectives and how it all comes together. Each parties’ goal will dictate their interactions and actions (e.g the objective of the bank, the client, the investors, etc etc).
