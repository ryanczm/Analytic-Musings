Progress:

So the nice thing is that I have prepared a list of statistics books divided into 3 sub-areas: Probability, general statistics and distributions, linear modelling and Bayesian statistics. Technically time series was originally part of statistics, but I grouped it under ML for organization purposes. As of this point, I'm starting on the linear modelling part, having finished the first part.

However, I also question the effectiveness of traditional statistical methods in problems like regression (OLS) versus the relatively newer ML methods of doing so, like tree-based models and neural networks. I was actually quite inspired by this book I found, Graham Giller's Adventures in Financial Data Science, and I couldn't understand a single statistical concept inside, so I said "ok time to read some statistics books".
